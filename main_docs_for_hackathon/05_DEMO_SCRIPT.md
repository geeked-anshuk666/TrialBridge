# TrialBridge — 05_DEMO_SCRIPT.md

## 3-minute demo, beat by beat

### 0:00–0:20 — The problem (20s)
"Over 80% of clinical trials fail to recruit enough patients on time — not
because eligible patients don't exist, but because nobody connects them.
Less than 1% of patients who qualify for a trial ever find it. TrialBridge
fixes that."

### 0:20–1:40 — Live demo (80s)
1. Type in Hindi (or use the language selector): a patient describing
   relapsing MS, having tried two disease-modifying therapies without
   success.
2. Show the detected-language indicator confirming Hindi automatically.
3. Show the pipeline status: "Searching 18 registries across 121
   countries..."
4. Six trial cards appear — rendered in Hindi.
5. Show the eligibility score badge on each card.
6. Show trial locations on the map: two in India, two in the UK, two in
   the US.
7. Click one card — full plain-language explanation appears in Hindi:
   what the trial tests, who qualifies, where it is, how to apply.
8. Toggle push notifications: "We'll notify you within 30 minutes when new
   matching MS trials open."
9. Click "Share with my doctor" — a PDF generates in English for the
   physician.

### 1:40–2:10 — Global scope (30s)
"TrialBridge covers ClinicalTrials.gov, the EU Clinical Trials Register,
ISRCTN in the UK, and WHO's international registry aggregator. New US
trials appear within 30 minutes of registration. The interface and
explanations work in 95-plus languages — the same tool serves a patient in
Mumbai, São Paulo, or Lagos identically."

### 2:10–2:40 — How it was built (30s)
Screen-record 20–30 seconds of the actual Codex build session. "Built
entirely with Codex and GPT-5.6 in under 48 hours."

### 2:40–3:00 — Close (20s)
"TrialBridge: because the trial that could save your life shouldn't be
invisible. Available as a PWA — works on any phone, anywhere in the world,
in any language."

## Presentation notes
- Have at least one non-English demo run rehearsed and pre-tested end to
  end before recording — this is your single biggest differentiator, don't
  leave it to chance on the day.
- If live translation quality varies, pick the strongest-performing
  language pair you tested for the actual recording.
