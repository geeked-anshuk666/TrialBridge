# TrialBridge — 02_COMPETITIVE_ANALYSIS.md

## Existing solution categories and why each falls short

### 1. ClinicalTrials.gov's own search interface
- English-only.
- Requires the user to already know medical terminology to construct a
  useful query and interpret eligibility criteria.
- Single-registry — does not surface EU, UK, or other international
  options even when they may be more accessible or better-fitting for the
  patient.
- Zero personalized explanation — returns raw eligibility criteria text,
  not "here is what this means for you."

### 2. TrialSpark and similar platforms
- B2B tools built for pharma sponsors to accelerate site activation and
  enrollment operations — not patient-facing discovery tools.
- Not designed for, or accessible to, an individual patient searching on
  their own behalf.

### 3. Patient advocacy nonprofits / condition-specific forums
- Provide valuable but manual, volunteer-driven, single-condition guidance.
- Cannot scale to cover all conditions, all countries, all languages.
- Response time is measured in days, not seconds.

### 4. Generic translation tools (Google Translate)
- Translates words, not clinical or bureaucratic intent.
- Does not rank or filter trials by eligibility fit.
- Does not know which trials are actively recruiting right now.

## What TrialBridge uniquely does
1. Multilingual **from the ground up** — the entire interaction, including
   the trial explanations, happens in the patient's own language, not just
   a translated interface shell.
2. Aggregates **multiple international registries in parallel**, so a
   patient discovers both domestic and international options in a single
   search.
3. Produces a **personalized eligibility score** per trial, not just a raw
   keyword match, based on the patient's actual treatment history and
   specifics.
4. Converts dense clinical eligibility criteria into **plain-language
   explanations** a non-clinician can act on immediately.
5. Offers **proactive push notifications** when new matching trials open —
   turning a one-time search into an ongoing safety net.
