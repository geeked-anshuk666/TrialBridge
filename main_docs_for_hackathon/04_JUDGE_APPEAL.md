# TrialBridge — 04_JUDGE_APPEAL.md

## Why judges will care (mapped to the 4 judging criteria)

### Technological Implementation
- A genuine multi-step agentic pipeline: language detection + profile
  extraction → parallel multi-registry querying → eligibility scoring →
  multilingual explanation generation → push notification registration.
- Combines LLM reasoning with real-time parallel calls to multiple external
  public APIs — demonstrates non-trivial systems integration, not a single
  prompt wrapper.
- Multilingual output is a genuinely harder bar than English-only demos —
  most competing hackathon projects will not attempt this.

### Design
- Trial cards rendered natively in the patient's detected language, a map
  showing trial locations across countries, visible eligibility score
  badges, and a one-tap "notify me" toggle — immediately legible to a judge
  even without reading every word.

### Potential Impact
- Directly addresses a documented, severe, global structural failure (80%+
  trial recruitment failure) with life-or-death stakes for patients with
  rare or treatment-resistant conditions.
- Global by construction — a patient in Mumbai, São Paulo, or Lagos is
  served identically to a patient in London or Chicago.

### Quality of Idea
- The insight is not "chat with a database of trials" — it's specifically
  that the barrier is *discovery and translation*, not eligibility itself,
  and that multilingual, multi-registry aggregation is the actual unlock.
  This is a sharper thesis than a generic "AI healthcare assistant."

## Standing-ovation demo beat
A patient types their condition in Hindi, and within seconds sees six
ranked trials — two in India, two in the UK, two in the US — each explained
back to them, in Hindi, in plain language they can act on immediately. That
moment makes the global, multilingual claim concrete rather than abstract.
