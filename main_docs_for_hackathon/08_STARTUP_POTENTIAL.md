# TrialBridge — 08_STARTUP_POTENTIAL.md

## Honest monetization model
- **B2C freemium:** free basic search; $9.99/month premium for full match
  history, unlimited notifications, and physician-report exports.
- **B2B (the real revenue path):** pharma sponsors running trials pay
  $30,000–$50,000 per enrolled patient industry-wide. Even routing a modest
  number of successfully-enrolled patients per year through TrialBridge
  justifies a per-referral fee arrangement of $500–$2,000 with sponsors.

## Realistic Year 1 revenue
$30K–$80K from B2C subscriptions plus the first pharma pilot relationship.
This is not a "becomes a billion-dollar company overnight" idea — it is a
realistic path to a $20M–$50M outcome as an acquisition target for a patient
advocacy organization or a pharma data/enrollment company, contingent on
building genuine trust and clinical credibility over time.

## Why this monetizes cleanly despite a free core product
The B2C side exists mainly for distribution and trust-building; the durable
revenue is B2B, aligned with an industry that already spends heavily
($30K–$50K per enrolled patient) to solve exactly this discovery problem —
TrialBridge is a cheaper, faster front door to the same outcome sponsors
already pay for.

## Path to scale / exit
Land 2–3 mid-size pharma sponsor relationships and the unit economics
improve substantially, since the B2B side scales without a proportional
increase in patient-side operating cost (the registry data is free
regardless of volume).

## What NOT to claim
Medical/legal disclaimers must be iron-clad in any real deployment —
TrialBridge surfaces potential matches, it does not make eligibility
determinations, and framing must never imply a guarantee of trial
enrollment.

## Post-hackathon build priorities if continued
1. Add more registries beyond the initial 3 (aim toward the full WHO ICTRP
   18-registry coverage).
2. Build out the physician-facing report into a proper clinical referral
   packet.
3. Add condition-specific glossary grounding to reduce translation drift
   for highly technical medical terms.
4. Formalize the pharma-sponsor referral relationship and pricing model.
